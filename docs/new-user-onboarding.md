# Welcome to ACE Dialer

Your work phone, on your computer.

ACE Dialer lets you make and receive your work calls, send texts, hear voicemail, and chat with teammates — all from your laptop. No physical phone needed. Calls work from anywhere with internet.

---

## 1. Install the app

### On a Mac
1. Download **ACE Dialer.dmg** from the link in your invite email.
2. Open the `.dmg` file (double-click).
3. Drag **ACE Dialer** to your **Applications** folder.
4. Open ACE Dialer from Applications or Spotlight (Cmd + Space → "ACE Dialer").
5. On first launch, macOS may ask for microphone permission. Click **OK** — calls need this.

### On Windows
1. Download **ACE Dialer Setup.exe** from the link in your invite email.
2. Run the installer.
3. **If Windows shows a blue "Windows protected your PC" screen:**
   - This is normal for a new app.
   - Click **More info** → **Run anyway**.
   - You'll only see this once.
4. The installer creates a desktop shortcut and Start Menu entry.

## 2. Sign in with Microsoft

1. Open ACE Dialer.
2. Click **Sign in with Microsoft**.
3. Your browser opens — use your normal ApTask `@aptask.com` account.
4. Approve any permission prompts your IT may have set up.
5. ACE Dialer takes over again and lands you on the keypad. You're in.

> **You only sign in once.** Future launches go straight to the keypad.

---

## 3. Make your first call

1. On the keypad, type a phone number — or tap the contacts icon (top right) to pick from Recents.
2. Tap the green phone button.
3. To hang up: tap the red phone-down button.

**During a call** you can mute, hold, transfer, add a third person (3-way), and switch audio devices. Everything is on the in-call screen.

## 4. Receive a call

When someone calls your number, a full-screen ringer appears. You'll see three buttons:

- 🔴 **Decline** — sends them to voicemail
- 🟡 **Reply** — declines AND sends them a quick text (e.g. "On my way", "Can't talk, will text you")
- 🟢 **Accept** — answers the call

If the app window is hidden, a small popup appears so you don't miss the call.

---

## 5. Find your way around

The bottom of the app has these tabs:

| Icon | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Keypad** | Make calls, enter a number, dial extensions |
| **Recents** | Every call you've made or received. Tap a row to call back, or use the action buttons for SMS / favorite / block. |
| **Messages** | Two-way SMS / MMS with anyone. Send pictures, quick replies, attachments. |
| **Voicemail** | Listen, read transcripts (when available), reply with a call or text. |
| **Favorites** | Star contacts so they're one tap away. **Favorites sync across all your devices.** |
| **Chat** | Internal chat with other ACE Dialer teammates (no SMS charges). |

The top-right avatar opens **Settings** — notification preferences, light/dark theme, blocked numbers, quick replies, hold music, and more.

## 6. Updates happen automatically

You don't have to do anything to stay current. About once per release, you'll see a banner at the top:

> **Update ready — Restart to install**

Click the button when convenient. The app closes, installs the new version, and reopens. Takes about 10 seconds. **No re-download, no re-install, no SmartScreen prompts.**

## 7. Tips that save time

- **Microphone not working?** macOS → System Settings → Privacy & Security → Microphone → enable ACE Dialer. Windows usually auto-grants on install.
- **Calls drop or sound garbled?** Check the SIP status pill in the top-left of the app. Green dot = good. Yellow or red = your internet may be unstable.
- **Your DID number** (the number people see when you call) is shown in **Settings → Account**. If it's wrong, contact IT.
- **Quick replies** are SMS templates you can send with one tap. Edit them in **Settings → Quick replies**.
- **Block a number** from Recents (Ban icon on the row) or from a Messages thread (Ban icon in the header). They can't call or text you anymore.
- **Hold music** plays for the other party when you put them on hold. Upload your own clip in **Settings → Hold music**.
